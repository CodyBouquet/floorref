About Flooring Reference

A simple explanation-first resource for understanding flooring specifications.

Flooring Reference was created to make common flooring specifications easier to understand. Many product listings, quotes, and marketing materials use technical terms and measurements that can be confusing or inconsistently explained.

This site focuses on explaining what those terms mean — such as wear layer thickness, total thickness, plank width, and material differences — using plain language and neutral descriptions.

The goal is clarity, not persuasion. Content on this site is designed to help readers understand terminology and specifications they may encounter, without offering installation guidance, product recommendations, or buying advice.

By providing clear, straightforward explanations, Flooring Reference aims to empower consumers, contractors, and industry professionals to make more informed decisions when researching flooring options.

What This Site Is — and Isn’t

What it is

  • A reference for flooring terminology and specifications
  • Simple explanations of common measurements and labels
  • Neutral, brand-agnostic content

What it is not

  • Installation instructions or professional advice
  • Product or brand recommendations
  • A substitute for manufacturer documentation

How Content Is Created

Content on this site is created by reviewing commonly used industry terms, publicly available specifications, and general reference material. Explanations are written to be broadly applicable and easy to understand.

Because flooring materials and specifications can vary by manufacturer and product, information provided here should be considered general reference rather than definitive guidance. Please consult a flooring professional for guidance specific to your project.

Advertising & Sustainability

To support the ongoing maintenance of the site, Flooring Reference may display advertising. Advertising does not influence how topics are selected or how information is presented.
