Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Installation details are the guardrails that keep a floor within warranty coverage.

  • These requirements often depend on room size, sunlight exposure, and temperature ranges, not just the product itself.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Installation Checklist

  • Check the maximum run length and doorway requirements.
  • Plan transitions at changes in flooring material or elevation.
  • Confirm required molding or profile types.
  • Allow expansion gaps beneath transitions.
  • Coordinate transitions with baseboard or trim details.

Common Pitfalls

  • Skipping transitions in open-concept spaces.
  • Installing rigid transitions that block movement.
  • Assuming glue-down rules apply to floating floors.

FAQ

Are transitions always required at doorways? ⌄

Many manufacturers require them at doorways or between rooms, but requirements vary.

Can I use T-moldings instead of breaks? ⌄

A T-molding is a type of transition, but it must allow for movement as specified.
