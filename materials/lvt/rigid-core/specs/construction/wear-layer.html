Quick Explainers

What it is

Wear layer is the clear protective layer on top of the printed design that helps resist wear, scratches, and stains.

Why it matters for LVT

Wear layer thickness influences how the surface holds up over time, but it should be read alongside coating and core specs.

Related specs to compare

Compare wear layer with surface coating, traffic class, and warranty terms for a realistic durability picture.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Confirm wear layer thickness in mils (1 mil = 0.001 in).
  • Check if thickness is listed as nominal or minimum.
  • Review surface coating type and stain resistance ratings.
  • Match wear layer to traffic class and warranty coverage.
  • Confirm total thickness to understand the full build.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming thicker wear layer guarantees better overall performance.
  • Comparing wear layer in mils to total thickness in mm.
  • Ignoring coating or maintenance requirements.

FAQ

Is a 20 mil wear layer always commercial grade? ⌄

Not necessarily. Traffic class and warranty terms provide better context.

Does wear layer affect indentation resistance? ⌄

Not much. Indentation is more tied to core and backing support.
