Core Type Explained SPC vs. WPC · Rigid Core LVT · What the Core Actually Does

Core type is one of the most commonly cited specs in rigid-core LVT, but the SPC/WPC distinction gets simplified or misrepresented in marketing. Understanding what each core is made of, how they differ in practice, and what trade-offs each brings is the foundation for reading LVT data sheets accurately. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

SPC (Stone Plastic Composite) uses limestone and PVC for a denser, harder, more thermally stable core — typically 4–6mm total product thickness. WPC (Wood Plastic Composite) uses wood fiber and PVC for a softer, thicker, slightly warmer-feeling product — typically 6–9mm total. Neither is universally superior; trade-offs between density, feel, and stability depend on the specific application.

SPC: Stone Plastic Composite

What it's made of

SPC cores are formulated primarily from limestone powder (calcium carbonate) and PVC resin, compressed into a dense, rigid slab. The high mineral content — often 60–70% of the core by weight — is what makes SPC significantly harder and heavier than WPC. Typical SPC core density is around 1.8–2.0 g/cm³, compared to WPC at approximately 0.9–1.2 g/cm³. This density difference is why SPC is noticeably heavier to handle at the same plank dimensions.

Dimensional stability

SPC's high mineral content makes it significantly more dimensionally stable than WPC under temperature changes. Where flexible LVT might expand and contract several millimeters across a room-sized installation, SPC moves very little. This stability is why SPC is the preferred core type for radiant heat installations (where surface temperature fluctuates), large open areas, and spaces that experience pronounced seasonal temperature swings. Expansion gaps are still required, but the movement SPC must accommodate is much less than flexible vinyl.

Feel and typical thickness

Because SPC is so dense, it feels firm underfoot — similar to a thin stone or tile feel rather than the softer compression of carpet or cushioned vinyl. Total product thickness for SPC-core LVT typically ranges from 4–6mm, with pre-attached underlayment adding 1–2mm more. Thinner overall profile can be an advantage at transitions between flooring types, and the density also supports locking joint strength.

WPC: Wood Plastic Composite

What it's made of

WPC cores blend wood flour (finely ground wood fiber) with PVC resin and foaming agents, creating a lighter, slightly cellular structure compared to SPC. The wood fiber content gives WPC a different feel and acoustic property than the pure stone-and-PVC composition of SPC. Because of the foaming in many WPC formulations, the core has some inherent cushioning — which is why WPC products feel warmer and softer underfoot than equivalent SPC products without added underlayment.

Thickness and comfort

WPC products are typically thicker than SPC — 6–9mm total product thickness is common, sometimes with a built-in foam underlayment layer. This greater thickness contributes to a more substantial underfoot feel and slightly better impact sound absorption compared to thin SPC. For spaces where comfort underfoot is a priority — such as bedrooms or living areas where people stand or walk barefoot — the WPC thickness and foam layer are relevant factors.

Stability trade-offs

WPC is less dimensionally stable than SPC under temperature changes, though it is still far more stable than flexible LVT. In spaces with large temperature swings or radiant heat systems, WPC requires more careful management of expansion gaps and climate conditions. The wood fiber content is also a consideration in very high humidity environments — WPC cores are not inherently moisture-resistant in the way SPC's limestone core is, though the PVC surrounding the fiber provides substantial protection.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Note whether core type is listed as SPC, WPC, or a proprietary name — many brands use trade names that don't reveal the composition.
  • Check total product thickness alongside core type — SPC tends to be thinner overall, WPC thicker.
  • Review dimensional stability data if available — this is a more direct measure of performance than the core label alone.
  • If radiant heat is a factor, verify the manufacturer explicitly approves the product for that use regardless of core type.
  • Review whether pre-attached underlayment is included — this affects total height at transitions and whether additional underlay is permitted.

FAQ

Is SPC always better than WPC? ⌄

Not inherently. SPC is denser and more dimensionally stable under temperature swings, which makes it perform better in spaces with significant climate variation or radiant heat. WPC is thicker and typically softer underfoot, which some people prefer in residential settings. The right core type depends on the specific use case — both formats have valid applications, and quality varies within each category.

Does core type matter for glue-down LVT? ⌄

Glue-down LVT is typically a flexible format, so the SPC/WPC rigid-core distinction doesn't apply the same way. Rigid-core products are floating-floor formats. Glue-down LVT has its own construction specs — flexibility, dimensional stability, and backing type are the relevant specs to check for adhesive-installed products.

What does SPC stand for? ⌄

SPC stands for Stone Plastic Composite (sometimes Stone Polymer Composite). It refers to a rigid core formulation made primarily from limestone powder and PVC resin. The high mineral content makes SPC denser and harder than WPC, and significantly more dimensionally stable under temperature changes. SPC cores typically have a density of around 1.8–2.0 g/cm³.

What does WPC stand for? ⌄

WPC stands for Wood Plastic Composite. It combines wood flour (fine wood fiber) with PVC resin, creating a lighter, slightly softer core than SPC. WPC products are typically thicker (6–9mm total) and have a warmer, slightly more cushioned feel underfoot. The lower density also means WPC is somewhat less thermally stable than SPC, though it still outperforms flexible LVT significantly.
