Attached Underlayment Explained Rigid Core LVT • Built-In Pad • Acoustics

Quick Explainers

What it is

Attached underlayment is a factory-laminated pad on the back of some rigid core LVT planks or tiles that adds cushioning and can influence acoustics and feel underfoot.

Why it matters for LVT

Pad thickness and density affect sound ratings, transition heights, and whether additional underlayment is allowed or prohibited.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Note pad material (foam, cork, IXPE, etc.) and thickness.
  • Check IIC/STC ratings and the test assembly used.
  • Confirm whether extra underlayment is allowed or voids warranty.
  • Account for height changes at transitions and doors.
  • Verify compressive strength if heavy loads are expected.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming attached pad guarantees high sound ratings in every build-up.
  • Adding a second underlayment when the manufacturer forbids it.
  • Ignoring pad density differences that affect rolling loads.

FAQ

Can I add another underlayment on top of an attached pad? ⌄

Some rigid core products allow it, but many do not. Check the underlayment rules for the specific product.

Does a thicker pad always mean quieter floors? ⌄

Not always. Sound ratings depend on the full assembly and test method, not just pad thickness.
