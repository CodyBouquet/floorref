Quick Explainers

What it is

Edge profile describes the shape of the plank or tile edges (square, micro-bevel, painted bevel), which affects seam visibility and cleaning.

Why it matters for LVT

Edge shape influences the finished look, how light catches seams, and how tolerant the floor is to minor height variation.

Related specs to compare

Review plank size, locking system, and surface coating to align aesthetics with performance expectations.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Identify the profile: square, micro-bevel, or enhanced bevel.
  • Check if the bevel is painted or pressed into the pattern.
  • Confirm compatibility with click-lock or glue-down systems.
  • Consider maintenance implications for beveled edges.
  • Match profile with pattern repeat and visual goals.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming beveled edges hide subfloor irregularities.
  • Expecting square edges to look seamless on imperfect slabs.
  • Overlooking cleaning requirements in deep bevels.

FAQ

Do beveled edges make the floor look more realistic? ⌄

Often yes, but the visual effect depends on pattern and plank size.

Is edge profile a structural spec? ⌄

Not directly—it is primarily visual, though it can influence seam perception.
