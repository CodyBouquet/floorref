Plank size refers to the nominal length and width of LVT planks, often listed in inches or millimeters. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Larger planks create a more open look but may require flatter subfloors and careful layout to minimize lippage or telegraphing. Pair plank size with total thickness, subfloor tolerance, and pattern repeat to plan layout and installation.

Quick Explainers

What it is

Plank size refers to the nominal length and width of LVT planks, often listed in inches or millimeters.

Why it matters for LVT

Larger planks create a more open look but may require flatter subfloors and careful layout to minimize lippage or telegraphing.

Related specs to compare

Pair plank size with total thickness, subfloor tolerance, and pattern repeat to plan layout and installation.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Confirm the exact length and width in consistent units.
  • Check allowable dimensional tolerances if listed.
  • Review subfloor flatness requirements for wider or longer planks.
  • Consider pattern repeat and visual scale for the room.
  • Match size with carton coverage and waste factors.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming all SKUs in a line have identical dimensions.
  • Ignoring the impact of long planks on telegraphing or seams.
  • Using plank size to infer thickness without checking the spec.

FAQ

Do bigger planks mean better quality? ⌄

No. Plank size is an aesthetic choice, not a quality indicator.

Are sizes nominal or exact? ⌄

Sizes are often nominal; check tolerance or actual size if precision matters.
