Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Warranty detail sections outline what is covered and the conditions required.

  • Spec sheets often summarize warranty length, coverage type (residential vs commercial), and whether coverage is prorated.
  • Look for exclusions tied to moisture, maintenance routines, rolling loads, and use-class limitations, since those are the most common claim blockers.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Warranty Checklist

  • Confirm the warranty length and whether it is prorated.
  • Check exclusions related to improper installation or moisture.
  • Verify that approved underlayment and accessories are required.
  • Review claims process and documentation needs.
  • Match coverage to intended use class.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming structural warranty includes surface wear or scratches.
  • Ignoring installation conditions tied to coverage.
  • Using incompatible underlayment or transitions.

FAQ

Does structural warranty cover locking system failures? ⌄

Often yes, but only when installation and subfloor conditions meet requirements.

Is structural warranty different from wear warranty? ⌄

Yes. Wear warranty covers surface wear; structural covers core or joint issues.
