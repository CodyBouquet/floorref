Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Warranty detail sections outline what is covered and the conditions required.

  • Spec sheets often summarize warranty length, coverage type (residential vs commercial), and whether coverage is prorated.
  • Look for exclusions tied to moisture, maintenance routines, rolling loads, and use-class limitations, since those are the most common claim blockers.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Warranty Checklist

  • Confirm whether coverage is for topical water only.
  • Check the duration and any time limits for standing water.
  • Review exclusions like flooding or subfloor moisture.
  • Verify installation requirements such as sealing or transitions.
  • Follow maintenance guidelines to keep coverage intact.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming waterproof warranty covers subfloor moisture issues.
  • Leaving standing water beyond the allowed time.
  • Ignoring required expansion gaps or transitions.

FAQ

Is waterproof warranty the same as water-resistant? ⌄

No. Terms vary by manufacturer and the coverage details matter.

Does waterproof mean the floor can be submerged? ⌄

Typically no. Most warranties only cover topical water exposure.
