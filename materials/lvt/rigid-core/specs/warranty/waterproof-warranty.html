Waterproof Warranty Explained Topical Water Coverage · Subfloor Moisture Exclusion · Wet Areas · Standing Water Limits

Waterproof warranty for rigid core LVT covers the planks' ability to resist damage from topical water exposure — spills, damp mopping, humidity, and surface moisture — without swelling, warping, or delaminating. It is a property warranty on the material itself, not a warranty against all water-related damage to the installation. Critical exclusions typically include subfloor moisture intrusion, flooding, standing water beyond specified limits, and water that enters beneath the floor assembly through unsealed perimeter gaps. Quick answer

Waterproof warranty covers the planks against topical water — spills, bathroom humidity, damp mopping. It does not cover subfloor moisture exceeding installation limits, flooding, or standing water that penetrates beneath the assembly. Perimeter gaps in wet areas should be sealed or covered. Always read the warranty exclusions rather than relying on "waterproof" marketing language.

What Waterproof Means for Rigid Core LVT

Why rigid core LVT is waterproof

Rigid core LVT planks are manufactured entirely from PVC and mineral compounds — none of which absorb water. Unlike wood-based flooring (hardwood, laminate, engineered wood) that swells, warps, and degrades when exposed to moisture, the PVC core, wear layer, print film, and backing of rigid core LVT are dimensionally stable when exposed to surface water. This inherent material property is the basis of the waterproof claim. A plank left exposed to a water spill for several hours will not swell or delaminate — the water remains on the surface or at most penetrates between joint lines without affecting the plank material itself. This property applies to SPC and WPC cores alike.

What the warranty commits to

A waterproof warranty is the manufacturer's commitment that the planks themselves will not swell, buckle, delaminate, or suffer material damage due to topical water exposure under normal conditions. It defines the scope of that commitment — for example, "will not swell, buckle, or delaminate due to topical water exposure for the life of the original owner's ownership" — and specifies conditions: the installation must have been completed per the installation guide, subfloor moisture must have been within specified limits at installation, perimeter gaps must be maintained, and the water exposure must be topical rather than from subfloor intrusion or flooding. The warranty does not promise that the overall installation will remain problem-free after any water event — it protects the plank material, not the assembly.

Key Exclusions

The most important exclusions from waterproof warranties for rigid core LVT are: subfloor moisture — water vapor or moisture rising through the concrete slab or wooden subfloor and accumulating beneath the floating assembly is excluded; the waterproof warranty covers only the planks, not the installation system; flooding — water intrusion events that submerge the floor or allow water to penetrate extensively beneath the floating assembly are excluded; standing water — in some warranties, prolonged standing water (beyond 24 hours or a similar specified duration) on the surface is excluded if it results in water penetrating the perimeter gaps; and installation defects — floors where perimeter gaps were not maintained, where water entry points were not sealed in wet areas, or where subfloor moisture limits were exceeded at installation are excluded from coverage.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Confirm the warranty duration for waterproof coverage — lifetime residential is common; commercial coverage for water resistance is typically a defined shorter term.
  • Check the specific exclusions: subfloor moisture, flooding, and standing water are almost universally excluded despite the "waterproof" label.
  • Verify whether wet-area installations (bathrooms, laundry rooms, kitchens) require any additional perimeter sealing or transition details to maintain warranty coverage.
  • Confirm that pre-installation subfloor moisture testing is required and that results were within specified limits — subfloor moisture exceeding limits at installation typically voids both the waterproof and structural warranty.
  • Note whether the waterproof warranty requires using specific grout, caulk, or transition products at perimeter edges in wet areas — some warranties specify sealant requirements for bathroom installations.

FAQ

Is a waterproof warranty the same as water-resistant? ⌄

These terms are not standardized across manufacturers — coverage details must be read from the warranty document. A "waterproof warranty" typically covers the floor surface against topical water damage. Neither term covers subfloor moisture intrusion from below, flooding, or extended standing water. Always read the specific warranty exclusions rather than relying on marketing terminology.

Does waterproof mean the floor can be submerged? ⌄

No. Waterproof warranty refers to the planks' ability to withstand topical water exposure — spills and surface moisture. Water penetrating beneath the floating floor assembly through joints or perimeter gaps can become trapped, causing subfloor issues requiring floor removal even if the planks themselves are undamaged. The waterproof warranty covers the plank material, not the overall assembly against water intrusion from any source.

Does waterproof warranty cover bathroom or kitchen installations? ⌄

Rigid core LVT is widely installed in kitchens and bathrooms, and waterproof warranty typically applies to these wet-area applications under normal use — humidity, steam, spills, and damp mopping. Flooding and extended standing water typically exceed coverage. Perimeter installation details matter: gaps at walls and thresholds must be sealed or covered to prevent water from reaching the subfloor. Warranty coverage does not substitute for proper perimeter sealing in wet areas.

What is the typical coverage period for a waterproof warranty? ⌄

Residential waterproof warranties are commonly stated as lifetime — covering the original purchaser for as long as they own the home. Some manufacturers specify a defined term (25 or 30 years). Commercial waterproof coverage, if offered, is typically a shorter defined period (5–10 years). Longer warranty periods reflect greater confidence in the material's long-term moisture resistance.
