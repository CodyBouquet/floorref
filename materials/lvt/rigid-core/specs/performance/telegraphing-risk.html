Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Performance detail sections explain how results were generated and what they actually indicate.

  • Telegraphing risk often relates to subfloor prep standards rather than a single numeric test value.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Performance Checklist

  • Confirm recommended subfloor preparation and patching materials.
  • Check thickness and core type that influence stiffness.
  • Review flatness tolerances and leveling requirements.
  • Consider larger planks that highlight irregularities.
  • Plan lighting to minimize visible imperfections.

Common Pitfalls

  • Relying on underlayment to hide major defects.
  • Skipping skim coat over patch lines or joints.
  • Assuming telegraphing only happens with thin products.

FAQ

Can rigid core prevent telegraphing? ⌄

Rigid core can reduce it, but subfloor prep is still critical.

Does embossing hide telegraphing? ⌄
