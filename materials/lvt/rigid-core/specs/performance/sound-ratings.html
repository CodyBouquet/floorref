Quick Explainers

What it is

Sound ratings such as IIC (impact insulation class) and STC (sound transmission class) quantify how a floor assembly reduces impact and airborne sound.

Why it matters for LVT

Acoustic performance is essential in multi-family or commercial spaces and depends on the full assembly, not just the LVT itself.

Related specs to compare

Check sound ratings alongside attached underlayment and underlayment rules to confirm the tested assembly.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Identify whether ratings are IIC, STC, or both.
  • Confirm the test standard and assembly details (slab, underlayment).
  • Check if results are lab or field ratings.
  • Verify if additional underlayment is required or prohibited.
  • Compare ratings with local building code requirements.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming the rating applies without matching the tested assembly.
  • Comparing lab ratings to field ratings directly.
  • Adding underlayment that changes the tested rating.

FAQ

Do higher IIC/STC numbers always mean better? ⌄

Generally yes, but the assembly must match the tested configuration for results to apply.

Can I increase sound ratings by adding pad? ⌄

Possibly, but only if the product allows it and the assembly is tested or approved.
