Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Performance detail sections explain how results were generated and what they actually indicate.

  • Indentation resistance is typically measured as residual indentation depth after a defined static load.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Performance Checklist

  • Find the test standard and load applied.
  • Check the reported indentation depth or pass/fail criteria.
  • Confirm whether results are for the full assembly or a single layer.
  • Review pad compressibility for rigid core products.
  • Match results with furniture protection guidance.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming indentation resistance equals scratch resistance.
  • Ignoring the effect of point loads like stiletto heels.
  • Skipping protective pads under heavy furniture.

FAQ

Does a thicker wear layer improve indentation resistance? ⌄

Not necessarily. Indentation is more tied to core density and backing support.

Is indentation resistance required for commercial spaces? ⌄

Many commercial specs require minimum thresholds, but verify the project requirements.
