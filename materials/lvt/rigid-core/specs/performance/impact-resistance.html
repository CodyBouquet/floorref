Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Performance detail sections explain how results were generated and what they actually indicate.

  • Impact resistance is often a pass/fail test with a specified drop height and weight, so verify the test method.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Performance Checklist

  • Look for the test method and drop height/weight used.
  • Check whether results apply to the full plank assembly.
  • Confirm any conditions like temperature or backing used.
  • Review core type and thickness that support impact resistance.
  • Pair with wear layer and coating specs for surface durability.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming impact resistance covers scratch or abrasion resistance.
  • Comparing results from different test methods.
  • Ignoring how subfloor stiffness affects impact outcomes.

FAQ

Does higher impact resistance prevent all dents? ⌄

No. It indicates better performance in defined tests, but heavy impacts can still cause damage.

Is impact resistance more critical for rigid core? ⌄

Rigid core constructions can benefit from higher impact resistance due to their stiffness.
