Carton Coverage Explained LVT Packaging • Square Footage • Estimating

Carton coverage lists the square footage (or square meters) of LVT included per box based on plank or tile size and pieces per carton. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Accurate coverage prevents shortages or overages in estimates and helps plan freight, staging, and waste factors. Use plank size, pattern repeat, and weight per carton alongside coverage to plan layout and logistics.

What it is

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Confirm unit (sq ft or sq m) and pieces per carton.
  • Verify if coverage is net (finished) or includes tongue/groove allowance.
  • Add waste factor based on room shape and pattern repeat.
  • Check carton weight for handling and pallet planning.
  • Match coverage to order increments and minimums.

Common Pitfalls

  • Mixing metric and imperial coverage units.
  • Ignoring extra waste for diagonal or herringbone layouts.
  • Assuming coverage is the same across related SKUs.

FAQ

Is carton coverage the same as net coverage after cuts? ⌄

No. Coverage is the raw area of planks/tiles in a box; you still need a waste factor for cuts.

Why does coverage vary between collections? ⌄

Different plank sizes and piece counts per carton change the total coverage.
