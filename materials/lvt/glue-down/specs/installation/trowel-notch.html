Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Installation details are the guardrails that keep a floor within warranty coverage.

  • Trowel notch is often listed with a spread rate per gallon or liter; worn trowels reduce film thickness.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Installation Checklist

  • Confirm the recommended notch size and shape.
  • Check the expected spread rate per gallon or liter.
  • Replace worn trowels to maintain notch dimensions.
  • Match notch size to adhesive type and substrate.
  • Verify required rolling procedures after placement.

Common Pitfalls

  • Using a worn trowel that reduces adhesive coverage.
  • Assuming one notch works for all adhesives.
  • Ignoring manufacturer-specific spread rates.

FAQ

Can I use a different trowel size to save adhesive? ⌄

No. Using the wrong notch can reduce bond strength and void warranty.

Do different adhesives require different notches? ⌄

Yes. Adhesive formulations often specify unique trowel sizes.
