Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Installation details are the guardrails that keep a floor within warranty coverage.

  • Open time is usually provided as a range that changes with temperature, humidity, and substrate porosity.
  • Spec sheets may separate working time, open time, and cure time—each affects installation sequencing.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Installation Checklist

  • Identify open time and working time ranges.
  • Check temperature and humidity assumptions.
  • Note whether open time differs for porous vs non-porous substrates.
  • Plan staging and crew size to stay within the window.
  • Confirm rolling or set time before traffic.

Common Pitfalls

  • Installing after the adhesive skins over.
  • Assuming open time is fixed regardless of climate.
  • Skipping adhesion checks during large installs.

FAQ

Is open time the same as cure time? ⌄

No. Open time is the placement window; cure time is when the adhesive reaches full strength.

Do all adhesives have the same open time? ⌄

No. Open time varies by formulation and environmental conditions.
