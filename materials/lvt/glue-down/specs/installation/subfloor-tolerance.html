Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Installation details are the guardrails that keep a floor within warranty coverage.

  • Flatness is usually given as a maximum deviation over a set length (for example 3/16 inch over 10 feet).
  • Rigid core often requires tighter tolerances than glue-down, so check the method that applies to the specific system.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Installation Checklist

  • Identify the flatness tolerance and measurement length.
  • Check if different tolerances apply to rigid core vs glue-down.
  • Plan patching or leveling before installation.
  • Verify moisture and pH requirements alongside flatness.
  • Document subfloor conditions for warranty protection.

Common Pitfalls

  • Using a shorter straightedge than specified.
  • Assuming underlayment fixes major flatness issues.
  • Ignoring transitions or expansion gaps around uneven areas.

FAQ

Does a thicker LVT hide subfloor issues? ⌄

Not necessarily. Rigid core still requires a flat substrate to avoid joint stress.

Is subfloor tolerance the same for every product? ⌄

No. Manufacturers set their own limits based on construction.
