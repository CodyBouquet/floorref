Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Performance detail sections explain how results were generated and what they actually indicate.

  • Slip resistance values depend on test standards; check whether results are for wet, dry, or both conditions.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Performance Checklist

  • Check which test standard is used (ANSI A137.1, ASTM D2047, etc.).
  • Note whether results are for wet, dry, or both conditions.
  • Confirm if the rating is for the finished floor or sample material.
  • Review texture and gloss levels that influence traction.
  • Match results to project safety requirements.

Common Pitfalls

  • Comparing COF values from different test methods.
  • Assuming higher COF always means easier cleaning.
  • Ignoring maintenance effects on traction.

FAQ

Is slip resistance the same as embossing? ⌄

No. Embossing affects texture, but slip resistance is measured by test standards.

Do COF ratings apply after finishing or waxing? ⌄

Only if tested in those conditions; maintenance can change results.
