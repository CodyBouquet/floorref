Performance focus

Dimensional stability measures how much an LVT plank or tile expands or contracts under heat, light, or humidity exposure during testing.

Performance specs describe how the floor behaves under controlled testing rather than how it looks.

How the results are interpreted

Better stability helps reduce gapping, peaking, or buckling—especially in large rooms or sunlit areas.

Compare like-for-like specs with the same test method, units, and installation system to avoid misleading conclusions.

Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Performance detail sections explain how results were generated and what they actually indicate.

  • Dimensional stability results usually appear as a percent change or mm change after conditioning at elevated temperature.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Performance Checklist

  • Look for the test method and conditioning temperature.
  • Check the reported percent change or mm change.
  • Note whether results apply to rigid core or glue-down systems.
  • Confirm expansion gap and transition requirements.
  • Review sunlight/heat exposure limitations.

Common Pitfalls

  • Comparing results from different test methods or conditioning temperatures.
  • Ignoring expansion gap guidance when stability numbers look low.
  • Assuming stability numbers replace site climate control requirements.

FAQ

Does dimensional stability guarantee no gaps? ⌄

No. It indicates performance under test conditions; installation and site conditions still matter.

Is stability more important for floating floors? ⌄

It is especially critical for floating rigid core installations where movement has fewer restraints.
