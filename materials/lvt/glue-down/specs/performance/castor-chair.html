Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Performance detail sections explain how results were generated and what they actually indicate.

  • Spec sheets typically list castor chair resistance near related performance or installation data to clarify how the product should be used.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Performance Checklist

  • Look for the test standard (e.g., EN 425/ISO 4918).
  • Check if the rating applies to the full system (floor + adhesive).
  • Review the wheel type and load used in the test.
  • Confirm any requirement for chair mats or protectors.
  • Pair with indentation resistance for heavy furniture guidance.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming a pass rating applies to all wheel types or loads.
  • Ignoring adhesive cure time before rolling traffic.
  • Using castor chair results to predict scratch resistance.

FAQ

Does castor chair resistance guarantee no damage? ⌄

No. It indicates performance under a defined test; real-world loads and wheels can differ.

Is this spec relevant for rigid core? ⌄

It is most common for glue-down or resilient products where adhesive performance is part of the system.
