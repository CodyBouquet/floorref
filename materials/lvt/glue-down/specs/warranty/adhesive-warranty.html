Adhesive warranty coverage describes what failures are covered when the specified adhesive system is used, and it typically ties coverage to approved products and installation methods. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Adhesive failures can cause debonding, gapping, or telegraphing, so understanding warranty scope helps align installation practices with manufacturer requirements. Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Confirm the approved adhesive list and required primers.
  • Verify substrate prep and moisture testing requirements.
  • Document open time, spread rate, and rolling per instructions.
  • Note exclusions (improper slab pH, contaminants, or high RH).
  • Check warranty duration and commercial vs residential coverage.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming a floor warranty covers adhesive performance by default.
  • Missing documentation required to make a claim.
  • Using third-party adhesives not listed by the manufacturer.

FAQ

Is adhesive warranty separate from the flooring warranty? ⌄

Often yes. Many manufacturers treat adhesive coverage as a distinct system warranty with its own requirements.

Do I need to keep batch numbers or receipts? ⌄

Most warranties require proof of adhesive type, lot numbers, and installation conditions, so keep records.
