Commercial Warranty Explained Glue-Down LVT · Duration · Use Class · Maintenance · Proration

The commercial warranty for glue-down LVT is the manufacturer's contractual commitment to cover specific types of product failure when the flooring is installed and used in a commercial environment per the manufacturer's requirements. Commercial warranties specify what is covered, for how long, under what conditions, and what is excluded. They are separate from the adhesive warranty and from any residential warranty that the same product may carry. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Commercial warranties typically run 5–15 years depending on product tier and wear layer thickness. Coverage is conditioned on using an approved adhesive, meeting moisture limits, and following a documented maintenance program. Many warranties are prorated after an initial full-coverage period. Commercial warranty duration is separate from traffic class rating — both should be verified.

What Commercial Warranty Covers

Typical covered failures

A commercial warranty for glue-down LVT typically covers manufacturing defects, wear-through of the wear layer under normal commercial use (defined by the warranty), and structural failure of the product under the conditions of the specified use class. Some warranties also cover edge lifting or delamination attributable to product defect — though delamination attributed to adhesive failure or moisture exceedance may fall under the adhesive warranty or be excluded entirely. Manufacturing defects covered often include print defects, layer separation, and dimensional stability failures that occur under normal conditions. Scratch, dent, and indentation damage caused by misuse, improper casters, or loads outside the product's rating are typically excluded.

Use class and warranty tie-in

Many commercial warranties specify a maximum use class or application type for which coverage applies. A product warranted for Class 32 general commercial use is not covered by the commercial warranty if installed in a Class 33 heavy commercial environment (high-traffic retail, healthcare corridor, institutional space). Similarly, a product installed in an outdoor or semi-outdoor application when warranted only for interior use would not be covered. The warranty's application restrictions should be matched to the actual intended use of the space — installing a product in a more demanding environment than its warranty allows shifts liability entirely to the installer or building owner.

Warranty Conditions and Maintenance Requirements

Commercial warranty coverage for glue-down LVT is conditioned on compliance with the manufacturer's installation requirements (approved adhesive, moisture limits, subfloor flatness, rolling procedure) and ongoing maintenance requirements. Maintenance conditions typically require regular dry dust mopping to remove abrasive grit before it can abrade the wear layer, periodic wet cleaning with a pH-neutral cleaner approved by the manufacturer, and prohibition on waxes, polishes, or any topical coating that the manufacturer has not approved. On commercial projects, documented maintenance logs may be required to support a warranty claim — a claim made years after installation where no maintenance records exist can be difficult to support. The specific maintenance requirements vary by manufacturer and product line and should be reviewed at the time of specification, not after installation.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Confirm the commercial warranty duration and whether it is fully covered (non-prorated) for the entire term or prorated after an initial period.
  • Verify the use class or application type covered by the warranty — confirm it matches the actual intended use of the space.
  • Review all installation conditions required for warranty validity: approved adhesive, moisture limits, pH range, rolling procedure.
  • Read the maintenance requirements — document what cleaning products are approved and what is prohibited.
  • Confirm whether the adhesive warranty is included in the commercial warranty or is a separate document with separate conditions.

FAQ

How long is a typical commercial warranty for glue-down LVT? ⌄

Commercial warranties typically range from 5 to 15 years depending on product tier and wear layer thickness. Entry-level commercial products with 12 mil wear layers may carry 5-year commercial warranties; heavy commercial products with 20–28 mil wear layers commonly carry 10–15 year warranties. Some manufacturers offer lifetime commercial warranties on premium products, subject to maintenance and use class conditions. Warranty duration alone does not define coverage value — the proration schedule and exclusions determine practical benefit as much as the term length.

Is commercial warranty the same as traffic class? ⌄

No. Traffic class (ISO 10874 / EN 685) is a standardized performance classification based on laboratory testing — it is objective and third-party verified. Commercial warranty is the manufacturer's contractual coverage commitment — it is not standardized, varies by manufacturer, and is self-defined. A product can carry a Class 33 rating with a 10-year warranty or with a 5-year warranty. Use class and commercial warranty term should both be verified independently when specifying for a project.

What maintenance is typically required to maintain commercial warranty coverage? ⌄

Typical maintenance requirements include regular dry dust mopping to remove abrasive grit, periodic wet cleaning with a pH-neutral cleaner from the manufacturer's approved list, and prohibition on waxes, oil-based soaps, or solvents. Some warranties require periodic commercial scrubbing. Documented maintenance logs may be required to support a future warranty claim. Failing to follow the maintenance program — even if the product is not visibly damaged — is common grounds for warranty denial.

Are commercial warranties prorated? ⌄

Many commercial warranties are fully covered (non-prorated) for an initial period — typically 1–5 years — and then transition to prorated coverage for the remainder of the term. Under proration, the manufacturer's liability decreases over time, so a claim in year 9 of a 10-year warranty may result in only a fraction of replacement cost being covered. The specific proration schedule is defined in the warranty document. A long warranty term with steep proration provides less practical coverage in later years than a shorter non-prorated warranty.
