Deep Dive

Construction snapshot

Surface coating refers to the clear finish applied over the wear layer, often urethane-based or enhanced with additives like ceramic beads.

This spec sits in the construction block because it influences how the plank is built and how it feels underfoot.

How it shows up on data sheets

Manufacturers often list surface coating next to the wear layer, total thickness, and backing details so the layer stack is easy to compare.

Compare like-for-like specs with the same test method, units, and installation system to avoid misleading conclusions.

Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Construction details show how the product is built and how the layers interact.

  • Coatings are often labeled as urethane/UV-cured or enhanced with additives (like ceramic), plus gloss level.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Construction Checklist

  • Check the type of coating (urethane, UV-cured, ceramic bead).
  • Review stain or chemical resistance ratings if provided.
  • Note gloss level or finish description.
  • Confirm maintenance and cleaning instructions.
  • Pair coating details with wear layer thickness.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming coating type alone determines wear performance.
  • Using harsh cleaners not approved for the coating.
  • Ignoring gloss differences between lots or collections.

FAQ

Does a ceramic coating mean the floor is scratch-proof? ⌄

No. It can improve resistance, but scratches can still occur.

Is surface coating the same as wear layer? ⌄

No. The coating sits on top of the wear layer as a finish.
