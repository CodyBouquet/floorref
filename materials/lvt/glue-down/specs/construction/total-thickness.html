Deep Dive

Construction snapshot

Total thickness is the combined thickness of all layers in an LVT plank or tile, typically listed in millimeters.

This spec sits in the construction block because it influences how the plank is built and how it feels underfoot.

How it shows up on data sheets

Manufacturers often list total thickness next to the wear layer, total thickness, and backing details so the layer stack is easy to compare.

Compare like-for-like specs with the same test method, units, and installation system to avoid misleading conclusions.

What to compare alongside it

Compare total thickness with wear layer, core type, and attached underlayment to understand the full build.

These companion specs confirm whether the construction supports the performance claims.

Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Construction details show how the product is built and how the layers interact.

  • Total thickness may or may not include attached underlayment, so confirm how the manufacturer reports it.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Construction Checklist

  • Confirm the total thickness and units (mm).
  • Separate wear layer thickness from overall thickness.
  • Check if attached underlayment is included in the total.
  • Plan transitions and height changes at adjacent flooring.
  • Pair thickness with core type and stability specs.

Common Pitfalls

  • Using total thickness as a proxy for durability.
  • Comparing products without checking what layers are included.
  • Ignoring doorway and appliance clearance constraints.

FAQ

Is thicker always better? ⌄

Not necessarily. Thickness is one factor, and construction quality matters too.

Why do some specs list mil and mm? ⌄

Mil usually describes wear layer; mm describes total thickness.
