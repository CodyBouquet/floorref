Wear Layer Explained Glue-Down LVT · Mil Thickness · Commercial vs Residential Ratings

The wear layer is the clear protective top layer of glue-down LVT that sits above the printed design film. Its thickness — measured in mils (thousandths of an inch) — determines how much abrasion resistance the floor offers before foot traffic wears through to the print. For glue-down LVT, which is frequently specified in commercial applications, wear layer thickness is one of the most consequential specs on the data sheet. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Wear layer thickness in mils indicates surface durability: 6 mil is entry-level residential, 12 mil is standard residential, 20 mil covers light-to-general commercial, and 28 mil is appropriate for heavy commercial. The wear layer protects the print film; it does not determine backing density, adhesive suitability, or sound performance.

What the Wear Layer Does

Role in glue-down LVT construction

Glue-down LVT is constructed in layers bonded directly to the subfloor with adhesive: a backing at the bottom, then the core (flexible PVC), then the printed design film, then the wear layer, and finally a surface coating on top. The wear layer is a clear PVC film that protects the print film from being abraded away by foot traffic, grit, and rolling loads. In glue-down applications, the floor moves with the subfloor rather than floating freely, which can concentrate stress in specific areas — making the wear layer's abrasion resistance critical in high-traffic zones like corridors, entrances, and commercial seating areas.

How mil thickness is reported

Wear layer thickness is reported in mils, where 1 mil = 0.001 inch (approximately 0.0254 mm). Some manufacturers report in millimeters instead — 12 mil ≈ 0.3 mm, 20 mil ≈ 0.5 mm, 28 mil ≈ 0.7 mm. These are measurements of the clear PVC wear layer alone, not the total product thickness. Total product thickness for glue-down LVT typically ranges from 2 mm to 5 mm and includes all layers; the wear layer accounts for a fraction of that total. Confirming units (mils vs mm) on any spec sheet is important since both are used and can be confused.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Confirm whether thickness is reported in mils or millimeters, and convert if needed for comparison.
  • Check if wear layer thickness is listed as nominal or minimum — some manufacturers list the minimum guaranteed thickness.
  • Match wear layer thickness to the traffic class and commercial use class for the intended space.
  • Check the surface coating type (UV urethane, ceramic-bead urethane, aluminum oxide) — coating quality compounds wear layer protection.
  • For commercial projects, confirm the traffic class (ISO 10874 Class 31, 32, or 33) — this is a tested performance rating that complements the wear layer thickness specification.

FAQ

Does wear layer thickness affect the warranty on glue-down LVT? ⌄

Yes. Wear warranty duration is typically correlated with wear layer thickness — thicker wear layers carry longer warranty terms because there is more material to protect against abrasion before wear-through occurs. A 12 mil wear layer commonly carries a residential lifetime warranty. A 20 mil wear layer may carry a 10–15 year commercial wear warranty. The exact terms vary by manufacturer, so the warranty document rather than the mil rating alone should be consulted for coverage details.

Is a thicker wear layer always better for glue-down LVT? ⌄

Thicker wear layers offer greater abrasion resistance and longer performance life under traffic, which is important in commercial glue-down installations. However, the wear layer alone does not determine overall floor durability — backing density, adhesive bond quality, and subfloor flatness all contribute to how well the floor performs. Matching wear layer thickness to the actual traffic class and use conditions is the more effective approach than always specifying the heaviest available.

What wear layer thickness is recommended for commercial glue-down LVT? ⌄

Light commercial (ISO use Class 31) installations are commonly served by 12 mil wear layers. General commercial (Class 32) typically requires 20 mil minimum. Heavy commercial (Class 33) environments — high-traffic retail, healthcare corridors, institutional spaces — typically specify 28 mil or heavier. The product's traffic class rating (tested per ISO 10874) is a more reliable indicator of commercial suitability than wear layer thickness alone, since the full construction affects real-world performance.

Does wear layer thickness matter more for glue-down than floating LVT? ⌄

Wear layer thickness is equally important for both installation methods since it determines surface abrasion resistance regardless of how the plank is installed. However, glue-down LVT is more commonly specified for heavy commercial applications where thicker wear layers (20–28 mil) are standard. The installation method does not change what the wear layer itself does — protect the print film from being worn through by foot traffic and abrasion.
