Quick Explainers

What it is

VOC certifications verify that a product meets emissions standards for volatile organic compounds (VOCs) under specific test protocols.

Why it matters for LVT

Many projects require certified low-emitting materials for occupant health and compliance with green building programs.

Related specs to compare

Review VOC certifications alongside phthalate-free claims, EPD/HPD documents, and recycled content.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Identify the certification program (FloorScore, GREENGUARD, etc.).
  • Check the certificate expiration and product scope.
  • Verify whether testing covers the full product or just components.
  • Confirm compliance with project requirements (LEED, WELL).
  • Keep documentation for submittals.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming a certificate applies to all colors or thicknesses.
  • Using outdated or expired certifications.
  • Confusing VOC certifications with recycled content or sustainability claims.

FAQ

Does VOC certification mean no odor? ⌄

Not necessarily. Certification limits emissions but does not guarantee the absence of odor.

Are VOC certifications required for residential installs? ⌄

They are often optional for residential, but required in many commercial specifications.
