Quick Explainers

What it is

EPDs (Environmental Product Declarations) summarize lifecycle impacts, while HPDs (Health Product Declarations) disclose material ingredients and health-related information.

Why it matters for LVT

These documents support transparency and sustainability requirements in commercial or green building projects.

Spec Sheet Checklist

  • Verify whether the document is product-specific or industry-average.
  • Check the program operator and validity dates.
  • Confirm the scope (cradle-to-gate vs full lifecycle).
  • Review disclosed ingredients and thresholds in the HPD.
  • Align documentation with project requirements (LEED, WELL, etc.).

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming an EPD is a certification of sustainability—it is a disclosure tool.
  • Overlooking expiration dates or outdated versions.
  • Comparing EPDs that use different system boundaries.

FAQ

Do EPDs mean a product is “green”? ⌄

No. EPDs report impacts; they do not certify that a product is environmentally superior.

Are HPDs required for all projects? ⌄

Not always, but many commercial projects request them for transparency.
