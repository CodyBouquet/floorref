Deep Dive

Documentation overview

Recycled content indicates the percentage of material in the product that comes from recycled sources, typically split into post-consumer and pre-consumer content.

Environmental specs are typically documentation-driven and tied to project submittals.

Why it matters for projects

Recycled content supports sustainability goals and may contribute to green building credits or project requirements.

Compare like-for-like specs with the same test method, units, and installation system to avoid misleading conclusions.

Related documentation

Review recycled content alongside EPD/HPD disclosures and VOC certifications.

Check dates, scope, and whether the document is product-specific.

Spec Sheet Details to Look For

Environmental detail sections clarify documentation scope and compliance boundaries.

  • Recycled content is often split into post-consumer and pre-consumer percentages, sometimes verified by third parties.
  • Confirm the unit of measure and whether the value is listed as nominal, minimum, or tested result.
  • Look for the test standard or method cited; results are only comparable when the method matches.
  • Check whether the value applies to the full assembly (plank + underlayment + subfloor) or a single layer.

Documentation Checklist

  • Check the reported percentages for post-consumer and pre-consumer content.
  • Confirm whether the claim applies to the full product or specific layers.
  • Look for third-party verification or certification.
  • Review whether the claim is per product or per collection.
  • Match claims to LEED/WELL documentation needs.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming recycled content guarantees lower VOCs or emissions.
  • Comparing claims that use different calculation methods.
  • Overlooking whether recycled content is regional or global.

FAQ

Is higher recycled content always better? ⌄

Not necessarily; it’s one metric among many and does not indicate performance.

Does recycled content affect durability? ⌄

It can vary by formulation, so performance should be evaluated separately.
