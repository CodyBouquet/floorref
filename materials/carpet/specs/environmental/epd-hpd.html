Carpet EPD & HPD, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

EPDs and HPDs are disclosure documents that summarize environmental and material health information. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

An EPD (Environmental Product Declaration) reports lifecycle impacts, while an HPD (Health Product Declaration) lists material ingredients and hazards. They are transparency tools, not performance ratings.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

EPDs provide standardized lifecycle assessment data such as global warming potential. HPDs provide ingredient disclosures and associated health hazards based on available data.

How it's reported

Specs may request an EPD and/or HPD with a current validity date. Some require product-specific EPDs, while others accept industry-wide declarations.

Why it matters

These documents support sustainability goals and procurement requirements. They do not indicate durability, warranty coverage, or installation suitability.

FAQ

Does an EPD mean a product is sustainable? ⌄

No. It discloses impacts; it does not certify the product as sustainable.

Are HPDs the same as ingredient lists? ⌄

HPDs include ingredient information and associated hazard data, but they may use ranges and may omit proprietary ingredients.

Do EPD/HPD requirements affect cost? ⌄

Products with disclosures may have different pricing due to documentation and verification, but impacts vary by manufacturer.
