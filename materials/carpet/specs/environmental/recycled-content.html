Carpet Recycled Content, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Recycled content indicates how much of the carpet’s material comes from reclaimed sources. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Recycled content is usually expressed as a percentage of total product weight, often split into post-consumer and pre-consumer content. It is a sustainability metric, not a performance rating.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Post-consumer content comes from used products, while pre-consumer content comes from manufacturing scrap. Both reduce virgin material use.

How it's reported

Manufacturers may list total recycled content and break it out by type. Some certifications require third-party verification of these percentages.

Why it matters

Higher recycled content can support LEED or sustainability goals, but it does not automatically mean better durability or lower emissions.

FAQ

Is post-consumer content better than pre-consumer? ⌄

Post-consumer content is often favored in sustainability scoring because it diverts waste from disposal, but both reduce virgin material use.

Does recycled content affect carpet performance? ⌄

It can, but performance depends on how the materials are processed and the overall construction.

Do adhesives count toward recycled content? ⌄

Usually not. Recycled content is typically reported for the carpet product itself, not the installation materials.
