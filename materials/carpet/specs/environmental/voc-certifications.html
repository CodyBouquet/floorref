Carpet VOC Certifications, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

VOC certifications indicate that a carpet meets third-party indoor air quality emission limits. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

VOC certifications (like CRI Green Label Plus or FloorScore) show that a carpet’s emissions fall within defined limits after standardized testing. They do not describe durability or appearance.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) are airborne chemicals that can off-gas from materials. Certifications verify that emissions stay below program thresholds.

How it's reported

Specs usually list the certification name, program version, and sometimes a certificate number. Look for the exact program required by a project’s sustainability goals.

Why it matters

VOC certifications can be necessary for LEED or other green building criteria, and for occupant comfort. They do not mean a product is “zero VOC.”

FAQ

Is Green Label Plus the same as FloorScore? ⌄

They are different programs with different test protocols, but both address indoor air quality emissions.

Do adhesives need VOC certifications too? ⌄

Often yes. Many projects require adhesives and cushion to meet VOC limits as well.

Does VOC certification affect cleaning requirements? ⌄

Not directly. It is an emissions metric, not a maintenance or performance spec.
