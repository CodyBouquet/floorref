Carpet Roll Width, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Roll width refers to the finished width of broadloom carpet, which affects seam layout and material waste. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Most broadloom carpets come in standard widths such as 12 ft or 15 ft. Wider rolls can reduce seams but may increase waste depending on room size.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Roll width is the side-to-side dimension of the carpet as manufactured. It determines how the carpet can be laid out across a space.

How it's reported

Specs list the nominal width, often with a tolerance. Some products may offer custom widths for large projects.

Why it matters

Choosing the right roll width can reduce seam count, improve appearance, and minimize waste. It also affects handling and transportation.

FAQ

Are 12-foot and 15-foot widths interchangeable? ⌄

They are different products; layout and seam placement will change with width.

Does wider carpet always reduce waste? ⌄

Not always. It depends on room shape and how the carpet is cut and seamed.

Do roll widths affect pricing? ⌄

They can. Wider goods may carry different pricing or availability.
