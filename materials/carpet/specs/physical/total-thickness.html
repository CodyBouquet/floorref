Carpet Total Thickness, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Total thickness is the full height of the carpet, including pile and backing. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Total thickness measures the overall height of carpet from the top of the pile to the bottom of the backing. It affects transitions, door clearance, and feel underfoot.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Carpet thickness includes the pile height plus backing layers. It is different from pile height alone and can vary significantly by construction.

How it's reported

Specs list total thickness in inches or millimeters, often with a tolerance. Some also provide pile height separately.

Why it matters

Thicker carpets can feel softer and may help with sound absorption, but they also impact transitions and rolling load performance.

FAQ

Is thicker carpet always better? ⌄

Not necessarily. Durability depends more on density and construction than thickness alone.

Does total thickness include cushion? ⌄

No. Total thickness typically refers to the carpet only, not a separate pad or underlayment.

Can thickness affect door clearance? ⌄

Yes. Higher pile carpets may require trimming doors or adjusting thresholds.
