Carpet Yarn Type, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Yarn type describes how carpet yarn is made and assembled, which influences texture, fuzzing, and durability. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Common yarn types include continuous filament (BCF) and staple yarns. Filament yarns resist fuzzing and pilling, while staple yarns can feel softer but may shed more.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Yarn type refers to whether fibers are extruded in continuous filaments or spun from shorter staple fibers. The yarn structure changes how the carpet looks and wears.

How it's reported

Specs may list BCF (bulk continuous filament), staple, or spun yarn. Some also note yarn denier or filament count.

Why it matters

Yarn type affects shedding, appearance retention, and hand feel. It is one of several factors alongside fiber type, twist, and density.

FAQ

Is BCF always better than staple? ⌄

Not always. BCF resists fuzzing, but staple yarns can offer different aesthetics and softness.

Does yarn type affect stain resistance? ⌄

Indirectly. Fiber type and dye method have more direct influence, but yarn structure can impact cleaning.

Can yarn type change texture retention? ⌄

Yes. Filament yarns generally maintain appearance better under traffic.
