Carpet Fiber Type, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Fiber type is one of the most important carpet specs because it drives durability, stain behavior, and cost. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Common carpet fibers include nylon, polyester (PET), triexta, wool, and olefin. Each has different strengths in resilience, stain resistance, and price.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Fiber type refers to the polymer or natural fiber used to make the yarn. Different fiber chemistries respond differently to traffic, stains, and cleaning.

How it's reported

Specs list the fiber type, and sometimes blend percentages. Branded fibers may appear alongside the generic type.

Why it matters

Choosing the right fiber type helps match carpet to the space’s needs. It should be considered alongside construction details like density, twist, and backing.

FAQ

Is nylon always the most durable? ⌄

Nylon is generally the most resilient synthetic fiber, but construction and density still matter.

Is wool better than synthetic? ⌄

Wool offers natural softness and resilience, but it has different maintenance and cost considerations.

Does fiber type determine stain resistance? ⌄

Fiber chemistry influences stain behavior, but treatments and dye methods also matter.
