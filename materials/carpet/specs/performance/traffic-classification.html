Carpet Traffic Classification, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Traffic classification indicates the level of use a carpet is designed to handle, from light residential to heavy commercial. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Traffic classification is a usage rating that helps match carpet to the expected wear level. It is a guide, not a guarantee of lifespan.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Carpet is often grouped into use categories such as light, medium, heavy, or extra heavy commercial. These classifications combine multiple performance factors like density, fiber type, and backing.

How it's reported

Manufacturers may use internal rating systems or reference programs such as ASTM D5848. Ratings are typically descriptive rather than a single numeric value.

Why it matters

Using a carpet outside its intended traffic classification can lead to premature wear and appearance loss. Classification should be read alongside warranty terms and maintenance requirements.

FAQ

Is traffic classification the same as warranty length? ⌄

No. Warranty terms vary by manufacturer and often include exclusions that are separate from use ratings.

Does higher density always mean heavier traffic rating? ⌄

Density helps, but fiber type, twist level, and backing also influence the overall classification.

Can residential carpet be used in light commercial spaces? ⌄

Sometimes, but it depends on the specific rating and warranty. Check the manufacturer’s use classification.
