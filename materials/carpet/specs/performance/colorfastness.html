Carpet Colorfastness, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Colorfastness describes how well carpet retains color when exposed to light, cleaning, and environmental factors. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Colorfastness indicates how resistant carpet color is to fading or color transfer. Higher ratings mean better resistance under specific test conditions.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Carpet color can shift or fade due to sunlight, cleaning agents, or atmospheric pollutants. Colorfastness summarizes how stable a carpet’s color is under those exposures.

How it's reported

Manufacturers may list ratings for lightfastness, ozone resistance, or wet/dry crocking using scales (often 1–5 or 1–8). Higher numbers indicate better performance.

Why it matters

Good colorfastness is important for sun-exposed areas and commercial settings with frequent cleaning. It does not guarantee no fading—exposure time and intensity still matter.

FAQ

Does solution-dyed fiber improve colorfastness? ⌄

Yes. Solution-dyed fibers tend to resist fading better because color is built into the polymer.

Is colorfastness affected by cleaning? ⌄

Aggressive or improper cleaning can affect dye stability, so maintenance guidance still matters.

Can high lightfastness prevent all sun fading? ⌄

No. It reduces the rate of fading but cannot eliminate color change over long exposure periods.
