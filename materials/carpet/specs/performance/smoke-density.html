Carpet Smoke Density, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Smoke density addresses how much smoke a carpet produces under a defined fire test, which can be part of code compliance in commercial projects. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Smoke density measures the amount of smoke produced during a standardized fire test. Lower smoke developed values are generally preferred and may be required by building codes.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

During fire testing, materials are exposed to heat or flame and the resulting smoke is measured. The smoke density value reflects how quickly and how much smoke is generated.

How it's reported

Specs may list smoke developed values tied to test methods such as ASTM E648 or ASTM E662. Requirements often appear as maximum values or classifications in code documents.

Why it matters

Smoke density affects visibility and evacuation safety during a fire event. It is often evaluated alongside flame spread or critical radiant flux requirements.

FAQ

Is smoke density the same as flame spread? ⌄

No. Flame spread measures how fast fire travels across a surface, while smoke density measures smoke production.

Are smoke density limits code-specific? ⌄

Yes. Commercial projects often reference specific code thresholds depending on occupancy type.

Does cushion or backing change smoke results? ⌄

Backing systems and adhesives can influence test results, so specifications usually require testing of the full assembly.
