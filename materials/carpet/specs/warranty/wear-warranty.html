Carpet Wear Warranty, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Wear warranties protect against excessive fiber loss or breakdown, not normal appearance changes. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Wear warranties usually cover loss of pile fiber beyond a defined threshold. They do not cover matting, shading, or changes in appearance from traffic.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Wear is typically defined as a percentage of fiber loss in a specific area. The warranty defines how wear is measured and what qualifies as a claim.

How it's reported

Warranty documents state the term length and the wear threshold (such as 10% or 20% fiber loss).

Why it matters

Wear warranties can sound generous but often have strict definitions. Appearance issues like matting or crushing usually fall under texture retention, not wear.

FAQ

Does wear warranty cover matting? ⌄

Usually not. Matting is considered texture retention or appearance change, which may be covered separately.

Are wear claims common? ⌄

They are less common because wear must meet specific thresholds and documentation requirements.

Does cleaning affect wear coverage? ⌄

Yes. Failure to follow required maintenance can void warranty claims.
