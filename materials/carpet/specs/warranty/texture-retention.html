Carpet Texture Retention Warranty, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Texture retention warranties address how well carpet retains its original look under traffic. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Texture retention warranties cover excessive matting or crushing beyond defined limits. They do not promise that carpet will never show traffic patterns.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Texture retention refers to how the carpet’s pile holds its shape and resilience. Warranties define acceptable changes in appearance over time.

How it's reported

Warranty documents specify coverage periods and the rating system used to judge appearance change (often 1–5 scales).

Why it matters

Texture retention is influenced by density, twist, and fiber type. Warranties help set expectations but still require proper maintenance.

FAQ

Is texture retention the same as wear? ⌄

No. Texture retention is about appearance change, while wear is about fiber loss.

Do loop pile carpets have texture retention warranties? ⌄

Some do, but the evaluation methods can differ for loop vs cut pile.

Can vacuuming patterns trigger a claim? ⌄

No. Normal tracking or vacuum marks are considered typical appearance changes.
