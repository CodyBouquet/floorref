Carpet Edge Ravel, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Edge ravel describes how well a carpet’s cut edge resists unraveling or fraying. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Edge ravel resistance indicates how much a cut carpet edge frays or unravels under handling. Better ravel resistance helps seams stay tight and clean.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

When carpet is cut, yarn can loosen from the backing. Edge ravel tests measure how much yarn pulls away under defined conditions.

How it's reported

Results may be reported as a ravel rating or as the amount of yarn loss over a test period. Lower ravel values indicate better performance.

Why it matters

Poor ravel resistance can lead to messy seams and installation challenges. It is especially important for patterned carpets and tight seam alignment.

FAQ

Is edge ravel related to tuft bind? ⌄

They are related but different. Tuft bind measures yarn retention overall; edge ravel focuses on cut edges.

Can installers improve edge ravel? ⌄

Proper cutting, sealing, and seam techniques help reduce raveling.

Does backing type influence edge ravel? ⌄

Yes. Stronger backing systems generally provide better edge stability.
