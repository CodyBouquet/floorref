Solid Hardwood Fasteners, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Fasteners secure solid hardwood planks to a wood subfloor in nail-down installations. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Fastener type and spacing affect holding power and squeak prevention.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Common fasteners include cleats or staples of specific gauges and lengths.

How it's reported

Specs list approved fastener types, sizes, and spacing requirements.

Why it matters

Using incorrect fasteners can lead to loose boards or noise issues.

FAQ

Are staples acceptable for solid hardwood? ⌄

Often yes, but some manufacturers require cleats for certain products.

Does fastener spacing matter? ⌄

Yes. Too wide or too tight spacing can cause movement or damage.

Can you glue and nail solid hardwood? ⌄

Sometimes, but follow the manufacturer’s guidance.
