Solid Hardwood Radiant Heat, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Solid hardwood can be more sensitive to radiant heat because it is a single piece of wood. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Compatibility depends on system type, temperature limits, and humidity control.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Specs list whether radiant heat is allowed and any maximum temperature requirements.

How it's reported

Ignoring radiant heat guidelines can cause excessive gapping or cracking.

Why it matters

Radiant heat requires careful moisture and temperature management with solid hardwood.

FAQ

Is solid hardwood compatible with radiant heat? ⌄

Some species and cuts may be approved, but many products have restrictions.

What temperature limits apply? ⌄

Many manufacturers cap surface temperatures around 80–85°F.

Does board width matter with radiant heat? ⌄

Yes. Wider boards can show more movement with heat.
