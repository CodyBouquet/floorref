Solid Hardwood Acclimation, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Acclimation is the process of letting flooring adjust to site temperature and humidity before installation. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Proper acclimation reduces the risk of gapping or cupping after installation.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Acclimation involves storing boards in the installation environment for a specified period.

How it's reported

Specs outline target moisture content and time ranges based on site conditions.

Why it matters

Even engineered floors can move, so acclimation and moisture testing remain important.

FAQ

Do engineered floors always need acclimation? ⌄

Most do, though some products may have specific instructions.

How long does acclimation take? ⌄

It varies by product and site conditions, often 48–72 hours or more.

Is moisture testing still required? ⌄

Yes. Acclimation is not a substitute for moisture testing.
