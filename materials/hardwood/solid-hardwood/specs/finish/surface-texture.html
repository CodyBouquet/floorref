Solid Hardwood Surface Texture, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Surface texture describes the tactile surface treatment applied for appearance and feel. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Textures can help hide wear and add visual character but may trap dust in deeper grooves.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Common textures include smooth, wire-brushed, hand-scraped, and distressed.

How it's reported

Specs list the texture or surface treatment name.

Why it matters

Texture impacts cleaning effort and visual consistency across boards.

FAQ

Does texture hide scratches? ⌄

Textured surfaces can mask minor wear better than smooth finishes.

Is textured flooring harder to clean? ⌄

It can be, especially with deeper grooves.

Does texture affect refinishing? ⌄

Textured surfaces may be partially sanded flat during refinishing.
