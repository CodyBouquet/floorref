Solid Hardwood Janka Hardness, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Janka hardness measures the force required to dent wood and is used to compare species. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Higher Janka numbers indicate harder species, but finish and construction still affect durability.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

The test presses a steel ball into the wood to a standardized depth.

How it's reported

Specs list Janka values by species; numbers can vary by data source and grading.

Why it matters

Janka helps compare species but does not predict scratch resistance of the finish.

FAQ

Does higher Janka mean better durability? ⌄

It indicates harder wood, but finish and maintenance still matter.

Why do Janka values differ? ⌄

Natural variation, moisture content, and source data can change values.

Is Janka used for engineered hardwood? ⌄

Janka applies to the veneer species, but overall performance depends on the full product.
