Solid Hardwood Species, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Species identifies the wood used in the top veneer, which determines color, grain, and hardness. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

The veneer species controls the visible appearance and contributes to hardness ratings like Janka.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Common species include oak, hickory, maple, walnut, and exotic options.

How it's reported

Specs list the veneer species and sometimes the botanical name for clarity.

Why it matters

Species affects appearance, color variation, and relative hardness, but finish and construction still matter.

FAQ

Does species change stability? ⌄

The core drives most stability, but different veneers can move slightly under humidity.

Are exotic species harder? ⌄

Some are, but hardness varies widely; check Janka values when comparing.

Is veneer species the same as core species? ⌄

Not necessarily. The core is often a different wood or composite.
