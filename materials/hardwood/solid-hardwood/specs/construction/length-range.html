Solid Hardwood Length Range, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Length range defines the shortest and longest board lengths included in a carton or run. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Random length boards create a natural look and reduce waste, but affect layout and seam planning.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Length range is the spread of board lengths supplied, often with an average or percentage mix.

How it's reported

Specs may list minimum/maximum lengths and average length, sometimes in inches or millimeters.

Why it matters

Longer lengths can look more premium and reduce seams, but may be harder to install in small rooms.

FAQ

Are longer boards always better? ⌄

Not always. Shorter boards can help in tight spaces and reduce cut waste.

Do length ranges affect price? ⌄

Often yes. Longer or more consistent lengths may cost more.

Will cartons include very short pieces? ⌄

Most include a range; check the spec for minimum length.
