Solid Hardwood Board Thickness, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Board thickness is the full thickness of the solid hardwood plank. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Thickness affects stability, feel underfoot, and how many refinishing cycles are possible.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Solid hardwood boards are commonly 3/4 inch, with thinner options for certain installations.

How it's reported

Specs list nominal thickness and sometimes milling tolerances.

Why it matters

Thicker boards can be refinished more times, but installation requirements still matter.

FAQ

Is 3/4 inch the standard? ⌄

Yes, it is the most common thickness for solid hardwood.

Can thin solid hardwood be refinished? ⌄

Yes, but fewer times due to less material.

Does thickness affect radiant heat? ⌄

Yes. Thicker boards can slow heat transfer.
