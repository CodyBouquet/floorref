Solid Hardwood Cut, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Cut refers to how boards are sawn from the log, which affects grain appearance and dimensional movement. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Plain-sawn, quarter-sawn, and rift-sawn cuts each have distinct grain patterns and stability traits.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Cut is determined by the orientation of growth rings relative to the board face.

How it's reported

Specs may list the cut or indicate if it is mixed or specific (like rift/quarter).

Why it matters

Cut impacts movement, appearance, and cost.

FAQ

Is quarter-sawn more stable? ⌄

Generally yes. It tends to move less across width than plain-sawn.

Why is rift/quarter more expensive? ⌄

It yields fewer boards per log and requires more selective milling.

Does cut affect hardness? ⌄

No. Hardness is tied to species, not cut.
