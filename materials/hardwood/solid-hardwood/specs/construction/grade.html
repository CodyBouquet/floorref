Solid Hardwood Wood Grade, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Wood grade describes the visual characteristics of the veneer, including knots, mineral streaks, and color variation. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Grade indicates the appearance range you can expect in the veneer, not the structural quality of the core.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Grades are typically categorized as clear/select, character, or rustic, depending on allowed natural features.

How it's reported

Specs may use manufacturer-specific grading or industry terms and may include photo ranges.

Why it matters

Grade helps set aesthetic expectations and affects yield and price, but it does not determine durability alone.

FAQ

Does a higher grade last longer? ⌄

Not necessarily. Grade is mostly about appearance, not performance.

Why do grades vary by brand? ⌄

Each manufacturer sets its own visual tolerances and naming conventions.

Can you mix grades? ⌄

Mixing grades changes the visual blend and may make the floor look inconsistent.
