Engineered Hardwood Veneer Thickness, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Veneer thickness is the thickness of the real wood wear layer on top of engineered hardwood. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Thicker veneers allow more sanding and refinishing, while thinner veneers limit renewal options.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Veneer thickness is measured in millimeters or fractions of an inch and can range widely.

How it's reported

Specs list veneer thickness directly, sometimes as a nominal range.

Why it matters

Veneer thickness influences lifespan expectations and refinishing capability more than total thickness.

FAQ

How thick is considered refinishable? ⌄

Many consider ~2–3 mm or more as refinishable, but results depend on sanding depth and finish system.

Does thicker veneer mean better stability? ⌄

Not necessarily. The core still drives most stability.

Can you refinish thin veneers? ⌄

Light screening may be possible, but full refinishing is limited on thin veneers.
