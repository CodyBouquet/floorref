Engineered Hardwood Dimensional Stability, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Dimensional stability describes how well the flooring resists expansion and contraction with humidity changes. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Engineered cores are designed to improve stability compared to solid wood.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

It reflects the product’s ability to maintain size and shape under moisture swings.

How it's reported

Specs may list stability testing results or performance claims.

Why it matters

Higher stability reduces gapping and cupping but does not eliminate movement entirely.

FAQ

Is engineered hardwood more stable than solid? ⌄

Generally yes, due to cross-laminated cores.

Does stability mean no acclimation? ⌄

No. Acclimation and moisture testing are still needed.

What affects stability? ⌄

Core type, ply construction, and environmental control.
