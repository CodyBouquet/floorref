Engineered Hardwood Milling Profile, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Milling profile describes how board edges are shaped for installation and alignment. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Milling profile affects how boards lock together, how seams align, and whether glue is required.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Profiles include tongue-and-groove, click-lock systems, and micro-beveled edges.

How it's reported

Specs list edge profile, locking method, and bevel details when applicable.

Why it matters

Milling influences installation speed, plank alignment, and seam visibility.

FAQ

Is click-lock only for floating floors? ⌄

Most click systems are designed for floating installs, though some can be glued.

Do bevels change performance? ⌄

They mainly affect appearance by emphasizing plank edges, not durability.

Can milling vary between batches? ⌄

Tolerances exist, but reputable products maintain consistent milling.
