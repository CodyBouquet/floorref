Engineered Hardwood Maximum Run Length, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Maximum run length is the longest continuous span recommended without expansion breaks. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Longer runs increase movement stress, especially in floating installations.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Manufacturers specify maximum length/width spans before expansion joints are required.

How it's reported

Specs list maximum run dimensions and any required expansion breaks.

Why it matters

Ignoring run limits can lead to buckling or joint failure.

FAQ

Does max run apply to glue-down floors? ⌄

Typically it matters more for floating floors, but check product rules.

Can transition strips serve as breaks? ⌄

Yes, if installed per manufacturer guidance.

What affects maximum run? ⌄

Room size, humidity swings, and installation method.
