Engineered Hardwood Installation Methods, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Engineered hardwood can be installed using different methods depending on product design and subfloor. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Common methods include floating (click), glue-down, and nail/staple-down.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Method depends on the core construction, subfloor type, and project conditions.

How it's reported

Specs list approved installation methods for each product.

Why it matters

Choosing the correct method affects stability, acoustics, and warranty coverage.

FAQ

Is floating installation best? ⌄

It is faster but may feel less solid; use when approved by the product.

Can engineered floors be nailed? ⌄

Many can, especially plywood-core products; check the specs.

Does installation method affect sound? ⌄

Yes. Floating floors often need underlayment for sound control.
