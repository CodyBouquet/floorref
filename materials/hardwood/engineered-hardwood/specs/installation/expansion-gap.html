Engineered Hardwood Expansion Gap, Explained What it means • How it's reported • Why it matters

Expansion gaps are the required clearances around the perimeter to allow wood movement. Reference-only: no product recommendations.

Quick answer

Even engineered floors expand and contract with humidity, so gaps prevent buckling.

What it is How it's reported Why it matters FAQ

What it is

Gaps are left at walls, thresholds, and fixed objects and covered by trim.

How it's reported

Specs or installation guides specify minimum gap widths.

Why it matters

Insufficient gaps can lead to peaking or buckling failures.

FAQ

How big should the gap be? ⌄

It depends on product and room size; check the manufacturer’s guide.

Do floating floors need larger gaps? ⌄

Often yes, because the floor moves as a single unit.

Can gaps be filled? ⌄

No. They must remain open or covered by trim to allow movement.
